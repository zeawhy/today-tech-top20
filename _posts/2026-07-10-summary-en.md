## [Long March 10B achieves world's first net-based rocket recovery](https://weibo.com/7340734455/R814of1Ki) ⭐️ 9.0/10

On July 10, 2026, the Long March 10B rocket launched from Hainan Commercial Space Launch Site and successfully recovered its first stage via a net-based system on a sea platform, marking the world's first net-based recovery of a launch vehicle and China's first controlled recovery of a rocket first stage. This breakthrough makes China the second country after the United States to master controlled sea recovery of rocket stages, significantly advancing reusable rocket technology and reducing launch costs. The net-based recovery method offers a novel alternative to traditional landing legs, potentially enabling more flexible and precise recovery operations. The first stage separated about 6 minutes after liftoff, then vertically returned and was captured by a net system on the recovery platform. The net system uses laser radar to monitor the rocket's position and attitude, while the rocket itself provides precise positioning data to coordinate the capture.

telegram · zaihuapd · Jul 10, 04:36

**Background**: Reusable rocket technology aims to recover and reuse the most expensive parts of a rocket, primarily the first stage, to reduce launch costs. Traditional recovery methods use landing legs or parachutes, while net-based recovery involves catching the rocket with a net-like system, similar to how arresting wires catch aircraft on aircraft carriers. China's Long March 10 series includes variants for crewed lunar missions and commercial launches, with the 10B being a commercial variant.

## [EU Finds Instagram and Facebook Addictive Design Breaches DSA](https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/home/en) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The European Commission has preliminarily found that Meta's Instagram and Facebook violate the Digital Services Act (DSA) due to their addictive design features, including infinite scroll, autoplay, and personalized recommendations. This marks a major regulatory action under the DSA, potentially forcing Meta to redesign core features of its platforms and setting a precedent for how social media companies must address addictive design across the EU. The Commission criticized Meta's time-limiting tools as ineffective and demanded default deactivation of addictive features, effective screen-time limits, and reduced engagement-driven algorithmic recommendations. If finalized, Meta could face fines up to approximately $12 billion (10% of global annual turnover).

**Background**: The Digital Services Act (DSA) is an EU regulation that came into force in 2022, establishing rules for online platforms to ensure transparency, accountability, and user safety. Addictive design refers to features like infinite scroll and personalized recommendations that exploit psychological vulnerabilities to maximize user engagement, often at the expense of mental health.

A Reddit discussion questions why the ML research community does not limit submissions per author, citing successful practices in fields like security (CCS) and computer architecture (DAC) to manage review workload. The massive volume of submissions in ML conferences degrades review quality, as seen in recent ARR cycles, and adopting submission limits could improve the peer review process across the entire field. The original poster notes that other research communities have successfully limited submissions per author for years, and wonders if there is a cultural reason for ML's different approach.

reddit · r/MachineLearning · /u/alafaya101 · Jul 10, 14:59

**Background**: The ML research community, especially in NLP through ACL Rolling Review (ARR), has seen a surge in submissions, leading to increased reviewer workload and concerns about review quality. ARR recently had to increase reciprocal service assignments for authors due to the high submission volume. In contrast, conferences like CCS (Computer and Communications Security) and DAC (Design Automation Conference) have long enforced submission limits per author to keep workloads manageable.

On July 10, 2026, China's Ministry of Commerce and General Administration of Customs jointly issued Announcement No. 29, imposing a temporary export ban on helium (HS code 2804290010) effective immediately. Helium is critical for semiconductor manufacturing, MRI machines, fiber optics, and aerospace, and China is a major supplier; this ban could disrupt global supply chains and exacerbate the ongoing helium shortage. The official notice provides no explanation, duration, or exemption mechanisms, leaving commercial and geopolitical rationale unclear. The ban covers only helium under HS code 2804290010, which is the primary industrial grade.

**Background**: Helium is a non-renewable resource essential for cooling superconducting magnets in MRI machines and as an inert gas in semiconductor fabrication. China has been a growing exporter of helium, and the global market has faced recurring supply shortages. The ban follows similar export controls on other critical materials like rare earths and gallium.
